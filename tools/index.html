The game

            

              Sigilry: The Braided Stair aims to blend the clarity of old-school
              glyph aesthetics with modern 3D readability—then put that look on top of a tight,
              loot-driven progression loop.
            

          


          

            

              

                
              

              
Braided vertical dungeon

              

                A stair-focused structure that forks, reconnects, and loops—so “route planning” is
                part of the build.
              

            


            

              

                
              

              
Sigils as systems

              

                Sigils aren’t just lore—they’re knobs. Stack them, twist them, and let them shape
                your runs.
              

            


            

              

                
              

              
Loot-first builds

              

                Find, fuse, and refine gear. The fun is in turning “junk” into a weird synergy that
                suddenly works.
              

            


            

              

                
              

              
Runes & modifiers

              

                Small modifiers matter. Runes change the rules of encounters and encourage
                experimentation.
              

            


            

              

                
              

              
Keys, locks, shortcuts

              

                Unlock routes and rooms. Your progress is spatial—not just numerical.
              

            


            

              

                
              

              
Dungeon atmosphere

              

                Gated chambers, ritual geometry, and “what is this place?” energy—wrapped in the
                ASCII-rendered aesthetic.
